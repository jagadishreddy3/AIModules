# LinkedIn Post: 7 Steps to an All-Star Profile for QA & SDETs 🌟

Struggling to land interviews as a QA Engineer or SDET in the AI era? Your LinkedIn profile might be the bottleneck. 🛑

A recruiter spends fewer than 10 seconds on your profile. If you don't have an "All-Star" status, you're missing out on significantly more opportunities. 

Here is the exact 7-step playbook to unlock LinkedIn All-Star status and make recruiters come to YOU:

1️⃣ **Profile Photo:** A professional headshot isn't optional—it makes you 11x more likely to be found. (Don't have one? Use AI to generate a professional shot!).
2️⃣ **Headline:** Don't just list your job title. Show your tech stack and value. 
*Example:* SDET Manager at Tekion | AI & LLM Test Architect | Playwright • Selenium
3️⃣ **About / Summary:** Be a "Builder." Tell your professional story, highlight your frameworks, and show the impact of your automation in ~150 words.
4️⃣ **Experience:** You need at least 3 job descriptions with full details to qualify. List your roles and focus on metrics: "Reduced test execution time by 40%..."
5️⃣ **Education:** Add your latest degree (e.g., B.Tech, 2009–2013). It builds credibility and triggers the alumni networking algorithm.
6️⃣ **Skills:** You need at least 5. Prioritize keywords that tech recruiters are actively searching for: Playwright, Selenium, Test Automation, Python, AI Testing, CI/CD.
7️⃣ **Connections:** You need at least 50 connections. Sync your email, connect with former colleagues from past companies, and engage with the testing community!
